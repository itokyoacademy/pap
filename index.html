Preamble: This document outlines the comprehensive terms and conditions ("Agreement") for the Pay After Placement (PAP) Program ("Program") offered by iTokyo Academy ("Academy"). Enrollment signifies acceptance of these terms.

Introduction: The PAP Program is designed to provide intensive training in specified IT skills and Japanese language proficiency, with the primary course fee payable by the student ("Student") only after securing employment, subject to the conditions herein.

1. Financial Commitment & PAP Model:

  • The Student agrees to pay the Academy a total course fee of One Lakh Fifty Thousand Indian Rupees (₹1,50,000) plus applicable Goods and Services Tax (currently 18% GST) ("Course Fee").
  • This Course Fee is payable by the Student to the Academy ONLY after the Student secures a job placement ("Placement") and receives their first month's salary, subject to adherence to all terms and conditions of this Agreement.
  • The previously mentioned Job Consultation Fee (equivalent to one month's gross salary) is NO LONGER APPLICABLE and has been removed from the PAP program structure.

2. Initial Fees (Non-Refundable):

  • A non-refundable Material & Resource Fee of Five Thousand Five Hundred Indian Rupees (INR 5,500) plus applicable GST must be paid by the Student upon registration and acceptance into the PAP program. This fee covers initial administrative and resource costs.
  • Official examination fees for Japanese Language Proficiency Test (JLPT) or any other third-party certification exams are separate and must be borne by the Student directly as per the respective examination body's charges and schedules.

3. Mandatory Assessments & PAP Continuation:

  • The Student must mandatorily appear for and successfully clear the following internal or external assessments as determined by the Academy:
    • Japanese Language N5 level (or equivalent) assessment by approximately the 3rd month of the program.
    • Japanese Language N4 level (or equivalent) assessment by approximately the 6th month of the program.
    • Japanese Language N3 level (or equivalent) assessment by approximately the 10th month of the program.
  • The PAP model, specifically the deferred payment of the Course Fee, is contingent upon the Student successfully clearing ALL the aforementioned assessments within the stipulated timelines or grace periods defined by the Academy.
  • If the Student fails to qualify in any of these mandatory assessments, the PAP model will cease to apply to the Student. The Student will then be required to pay the standard iTokyo Academy course fee, applicable at that time for the completed and ongoing training modules, should they wish to continue their studies with the Academy. In such a scenario, the Academy's obligations concerning deferred payment under the PAP terms would cease, and the Student would not be eligible for the deferred payment benefit for the Course Fee.

4. Attendance Requirement:

  • A minimum of 95% to 100% attendance in all scheduled classes, workshops, lab sessions, and other mandatory program-related activities is compulsory for the Student to remain in good standing and eligible for the benefits of the PAP program.
  • Consistent absenteeism without prior approval or valid reason may lead to warnings and, in persistent cases, disqualification from the PAP model as outlined under Program Discontinuation.

5. Code of Conduct & Disqualification:

  • The Student must adhere strictly to iTokyo Academy's code of conduct at all times. Prohibited behaviors include, but are not limited to: any form of harassment, academic dishonesty, requesting or lending money among students/staff, forming or participating in unauthorized groups or unions, engaging in inappropriate relationships with staff or other students, taking unauthorized leave, sharing personal contact information of staff/students without explicit consent, disruptive behavior, or any action that brings disrepute to the Academy.
  • Any act of misconduct, indiscipline, or violation of the code of conduct will be subject to disciplinary action, which may include immediate disqualification from the PAP program. Upon disqualification, the Student will forfeit any benefits under the PAP model and may become liable for the full standard course fees or a pro-rata portion thereof, as determined by the Academy.

6. Document Submission & Verification:

  • The Student must submit true and accurate copies of all required educational documents, identification proofs, and any other documents requested by the Academy for verification purposes at the time of admission or as required during the program.
  • A cancelled cheque leaf or bank account details for future payment processing (post-placement) will be required as per the formal PAP agreement.
  • Misrepresentation or submission of fraudulent documents will lead to immediate disqualification and potential legal action.

7. Parental/Guardian Consent:

  • Enrollment in the PAP program must be with the full knowledge, understanding, and explicit written consent of the Student's parent(s) or legal guardian(s), especially if the Student is below a certain age or as required by Academy policy. A formal consent form will be part of the enrollment process.

8. Job Search Responsibility & Student Cooperation:

  • The Student must actively participate in and diligently apply learnings from all career development workshops, resume building sessions, mock interviews, and company-specific preparation sessions provided by the Academy.
  • The primary responsibility for securing employment rests with the Student. The Student is expected to proactively search for job opportunities, apply to relevant positions, and attend interviews.
  • The Academy will provide guidance, resources, and support to enhance the Student's employability and job search effectiveness (e.g., interview coaching, resume feedback, industry insights). However, the Academy does not guarantee job placement or any specific number of interview opportunities.
  • Failure to actively participate in career readiness activities, demonstrate serious effort in the job search process, or unprofessional conduct during any part of the job application process may be considered a breach of program expectations and could affect ongoing support.

9. Relocation & Professional Conduct in Job Market:

  • The Student acknowledges that this Program is primarily geared towards preparing them for employment opportunities in Japan, and therefore must be prepared and willing to relocate to Japan if a suitable opportunity arises and is secured by the Student.
  • The Student is expected to conduct themselves professionally in all interactions with potential employers and to seriously consider any suitable job offers they secure through their own efforts or with guidance from the Academy. Unreasonable refusal of multiple suitable offers that meet generally accepted industry standards for entry-level roles (commensurate with the training provided) may be reviewed under the terms of the PAP agreement regarding student commitment.

10. Program Discontinuation / Breach of Agreement:

  • If the Student chooses to discontinue the Program for personal reasons after commencement, or breaches any material terms of this Agreement (including but not limited to attendance, code of conduct, assessment failures leading to PAP model cessation), they may be liable to pay a pro-rata amount of the standard course fee or the full standard course fee, depending on the stage of discontinuation and the nature of the breach, as detailed in the formal PAP agreement.
  • The Academy reserves the right to discontinue a Student from the Program for reasons outlined in this Agreement.

11. Governing Law & Jurisdiction:

  • This PAP Agreement and any disputes arising from or related to it shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of India.
  • All disputes, differences, or claims arising out of or in connection with this Agreement shall be subject to the exclusive jurisdiction of the competent courts in Chennai, Tamil Nadu, India.

12. Entire Agreement & Amendments:

  • The formal signed PAP Agreement, along with any annexures or schedules thereto, constitutes the entire understanding and agreement between the Student and iTokyo Academy concerning the PAP Program, superseding all prior discussions, negotiations, and representations, whether oral or written.
  • Any amendments, waivers, or modifications to this Agreement must be in writing and signed by duly authorized representatives of both the Student (and guardian, if applicable) and the Academy.
  • The information provided on the Academy's website or other promotional materials is for informational and illustrative purposes. In case of any discrepancy, the terms of the formal signed PAP Agreement will prevail.

By applying to and enrolling in the iTokyo Academy Pay After Placement Program, you (the Student, and your parent/guardian) acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to be bound by these terms and conditions and the detailed formal PAP agreement that you will sign upon selection.
